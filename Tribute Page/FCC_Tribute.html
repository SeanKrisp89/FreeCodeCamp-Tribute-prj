Walter Payton

"Sweetness"

The Greatest Bear to Play the Game

Walter Payton hurdled over defenders much like he hurdled his way into NFL history.

  • 1954 -Born to Peter and Alyn Payton in Columbia, Mississippi
  • 1971 -Walter began his college career playing running back for the Jackson State Tigers
  • 1972 -During his sophomore year, Walter sets a then-best single-game scording record by rushing for 7 touchdowns, (on runs of 6, 8, 2, 3, 1, 2, and 31 yards) and two 2-point conversions for 46 points as Jackson State defeated Lane College 72-0
  • He also set a school record with 279 rushing yards in that game
  • 1973 -Walter sets the school rushing touchdown record with 24 touchdowns in a single season
  • 1974 - Selected to the All-American Team
  • 1975 -Graduated from Jackson State with a degree in Communications and was drafted by the Chicago Bears in the 1st round
  • 1975 -Walter's first NFL game came with no success as he was held to zero net rushing yards on 8 carries. He would end the season with 679 yards and 7 touchdowns
  • 1976 -Eager to improve upon his rookie season, Payton rushes for 1,390 yards and 13 touchdowns. After the season, he was selected to play in the 1977 Pro Bowl where he was declared the Pro Bowl MVP
  • 1984 -Payton surpasses Franco Harris as the active leader in career rushing yards. Three weeks later, on October 7th against the New Orleans Saints, Payton broke Jim Brown's career rushing record of 12,312 yards.
  • 1985 -Payton records a Super Bowl victory with the rest of his Chicago Bears teammates, as well as goes on to record the widely known "Super Bowl Shuffle"
  • 1986 -At the end of the 1986 season, Walter announces that he will retire after the 1987 season
  • 1988 -Walter's career ends at the hands of the Washington Redskins in the divisional round of the playoffs
  • 1996 -Walter was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame
  • 1999 -Walter passes away from complications of cholangiocarcinoma (bile duct cancer)
“If you ask me how I want to be remembered, it is as a winner. You know what a winner is? A winner is somebody who has given his best effort, who has tried the hardest they possibly can, who has utilized every ounce of energy and strength within them to accomplish something. It doesn't mean that they accomplished it or failed, it means that they've given it their best. That's a winner.”

-- Walter Payton
